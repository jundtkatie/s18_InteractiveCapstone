It’s your grandpa’s birthday, and he wants to go out to the local steakhouse for dinner.

WHAT DO YOU CHOOSE TO DO?

Talk to your waiter

Throw a selfish fit and demand he choose a different restaurant

Many restaurants are happy to accommodate dietary preferences. The key here is to be clear and polite in explaining your diet. Ask if you can make a substitution to an existing menu item, or it a vegetarian dish can be made without the egg/dairy products.
A meal without meat is still a meal, and the notion that vegetables, starch, and other common components in the average omnivore diet are merely sides is simply untrue. Vegetables as a side to the meat is a frame of reference.
